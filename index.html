Brand
$1.25/
Mars, Nestlé, Mondelēz, Hershey, Lindt. They manufacture the bar, invest in marketing, and own the consumer relationship. The logo on the wrapper captures the second-largest share.
Mars · Nestlé · Mondelēz · Hershey · Lindt
The 3rd hand — The processor
Processor
$0.90/
Raw cocoa beans become cocoa mass, butter and powder here. Three companies — Barry Callebaut, Cargill, Olam — control 60% of all global cocoa processing.
Barry Callebaut · Cargill · Olam
The 2nd hand — The trader
Trader & Exporter
$0.70/
Commodity traders buy raw beans at origin prices and ship them to processors in Europe and North America. The value-add leaves the producing countries entirely.
Sucden · Touton · Olam commodity arm

The consequence — poverty
$0.23
per bar. What does that actually mean?
A farmer needs cocoa from 1,000 bars worth of pods to earn one day's living wage. Instead they earn $0.93 per person per day — below the World Bank extreme poverty line of $2.15. The chocolate industry earns $130 billion annually. The math is not complicated. It is a choice.

The consequence — child labour
$0.23 wasn't enough.
So the children went to work.
When a family cannot survive on cocoa income, the choice is devastating — the children join the farm. Not out of tradition. Not by accident. Out of necessity manufactured by the supply chain above them.
1.56 million children work on West African cocoa farms today. They carry 50kg sacks, wield machetes, and are exposed to pesticides. 95% work in hazardous conditions. The average age is 12.
Every bar you have ever eaten was touched by a child who should have been in school.

2001 → 2009 → 2019 → 2020 · Deadline markers at 2005, 2008, 2010, 2020

Each point on this line is a survey count of children working on cocoa farms in West Africa. The amber markers are deadlines set by the chocolate industry's own Harkin-Engel Protocol — a 2001 pledge to eliminate child labour. Every single deadline passed. The line kept rising. By 2020, there were 14 percentage points more child labourers than when the promise was made.
